Hungarian Pork Steak Gypsy-Style

Description

Cigánypecsenye (pronounced tzi-gany-pe-che-nyeh), or Hungarian Pork Steak Gypsy-Style, is a rustic and flavourful Hungarian dish where tenderized, marinated pork is pan-fried in bacon drippings. Foods cooked in Gypsy-Style are hearty and satisfying, and are a real treat in Hungarian cuisine!

Ingridients

  • 1 kg pork shoulder blade steak, boneless (or about 4 pieces), See Note (*)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp Hungarian paprika
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 250 g smoked bacon (Hungarian smoked bacon preferred, although sometimes difficult to find), See Note (**)
  • ½ cup vegetable oil (you may need more for frying depending on size of skillet and how much is absorbed by your meat while cooking)
  • ½ cups all-purpose flour (optional)

Steps

  1. each pork steak with a meat tenderizer. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika. Rub both sides with garlic, set aside in the fridge to marinate (about 1 hour).While your pork is marinating, begin cooking your bacon. If using Hungarian bacon, cut a about a 1 inch thick slice and cut bacon in a cockscomb shape (as seen in photo).
  2. >In a large skillet, cook your bacon until nice and crispy. Remove bacon from skillet and set aside. Do not discard bacon drippings from skillet.
  3. Add oil to same skillet. Heat oil in skillet with leftover bacon drippings.
  4. >Optional: If you're going to coat your pork chops in flour, do it now. You can either dredge them in flour, sprinkle some flour over top each side, or omit this step entirely.
  5. Place pork steaks in the skillet. Cook pork steaks in bacon drippings and oil, flipping half way through (about 4-7 minutes per side, depending on size and thickness of your meat). If using bone-in shoulder blade steak, you will need to pan-fry it longer, about 7-10 min per side.
  6. Serve with bacon on top and a side of potatoes.
